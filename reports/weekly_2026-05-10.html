    Saved at 0.48% on 381 reach. Same death pattern as last week's "CBO vs ABO… which is better?" (0.00%). A question without an implied answer in the hook gives the viewer no reason to save: there is no takeaway to hold for later.   • First-person reflective hooks like "I doubled my show rate when I implemented these three things…" and "When I started working less on my agency, I scaled quicker than ever before"
    Saved at 0.33% and 0.00%. The first-person opener flips the frame from "this is for you" to "this is about me." It worked when paired with a specific dollar number ("My agency does $75,000 a month") but dies when it's a vague growth claim. Open with the viewer's situation, not Noah's.

    Saved at 0.00% and 0.76%. Both name a problem but never promise what changes. The disqualifier structure ("you're cooked if you're not booking X") works because the next half is implicit: "I'm gonna show you." The bare principle hook stops at the diagnosis.
