Gender-coded clickbait pulls the algorithm but doesn't earn a save because there's no measurable outcome the viewer can come back for. Pattern to avoid: hooks that bait identity intrigue without naming what the viewer learns.
  • "this is the easiest thing you can do to get more bookings from your paid ads right now"
    Saved at 0.00% on 149 reach. Generic "easiest thing" framing with no number, mechanic, or contrast named in the hook. Already in the playbook banned-pattern list. The rule the data keeps confirming: name the actual answer in the hook line itself. "drop one specific filter from your lead form and your bookings double" beats "the easiest thing you can do."
  • "Most agency owners don't know what their bottleneck is and therefore are not scaling..."
    Saved at 0.88% on 443 reach. "Most agency owners" addresses a crowd, not a person. The viewer can opt out. The fix the writing rules already prescribe: speak directly to "you." Rewrite as "You probably don't know what your bottleneck is. That's why you're not scaling."
  • Reels under 45 seconds.
    The 0-30s band still saves at 0.26% median rolling, the 31-45s band at 0.73%. Together they sit far below the 46-60s peak of 1.80%. The 0.36% miss this week was 37s. Default new scripts to 60-75s. Push to 85-90s when the topic earns the runway (the roasting episodes were 77s and 87s, both hit). Never go under 45s on a tactical reel.

What the words mean
  • Reach. Number of people who saw the reel at all. Bigger reach = IG pushed it.
  • Save rate. % of people who hit Save. The strongest signal a reel actually resonated, way more meaningful than likes.
  • Completion rate. Average % of the reel watched. Not "watched to the end", average across all viewers.
  • Median (vs average). The middle reel: half scored higher, half lower. Used instead of average so one breakout doesn't distort the picture.
  • Rolling baseline. The median across every reel ever, qualified at 500+ reach. Anything 2x baseline = a hit.
  • Format / Pillar. What kind of reel it is: Green-Screen, Talking-Head, Podcast clip, etc. Set in the Notion tracker.
